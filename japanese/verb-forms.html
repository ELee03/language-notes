Verb Forms

The forms grammar patterns attach to. This is the missing bridge between a dictionary verb and actual dialogue.

Core Idea

A grammar point usually does not attach to a raw dictionary verb randomly. It asks for a specific shape: dictionary form, ます-stem, て-form, ない-form, past form, potential form, or conditional form.

話す example: 話はなす -> 話はなします -> 話はなさない -> 話はなせる. So yes: hana-shi-masu, hana-sa-nai, and hana-se-ru.

Godan Example: 話はなす

Godan verbs move the final kana across vowel rows. For 話す, the す changes to し, さ, せ, etc. depending on the form.

How To Read A Grammar Card

Pattern tells you the visible grammar. Attaches to tells you the form that must come immediately before it. If that field says “verb ます-stem + たい,” then 行く becomes 行きたい, not 行くたい.
